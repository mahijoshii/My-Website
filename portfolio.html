As a woman, I was never encouraged to pursue a career in engineering, until I went to an engineering conference for women at the University of Waterloo. From this event, I became inspired to pursue a career in STEM, and I also wanted to encourage other youth as well. I’ve always wanted to start a STEM club, but I didn’t know where to begin. My school seemed like a great place to start because I have a lot of resources and adults willing to help. I was so excited to start the club. Around 60 students in my school wanted to join the club and further their knowledge in STEM by joining me for some hands-on activities. I was blown away by the response. It felt like I had accomplished so much in such a short time, and I was so proud to be the founder of this club. For the upcoming school year, I have big plans as I intend to hold meetings with the female empowerment club, and the ABC club to encourage females and black youth to pursue STEM. I am planning to enroll in competitions, host STEM related events, and even have guest speakers talk to the students.
